  • What is the relationship between mindfulness and self awareness?

    • Mindfulness and awareness always complement each other. Mindfulness is the process of relating with individual situations directly, precisely, definitely. Mindfulness is like a microscope; it is neither an offensive nor a defensive weapon in relation to the germs we observe through it. The function of the microscope is just to clearly present what is there. Awareness is seeing the discovery of mindfulness. So mindfulness and awareness work together to bring acceptance of living situations as they are.

  • What did you take away from the "My Emotions are Not Me" section?

    • My emotions are not me - Shift from “I am angry” to “I feel angry” to “I experience anger in my body”. After training, you will see emotions going from existential “I am” to experiential “I feel”. You will eventually see emotions as physiological phenomena. This is the beginning of your mastery over your emotions. Emotions are like clouds in the sky – the clouds are not the sky; they are phenomena in the sky that comes and go.
